# Article Quality Standard

Use this standard before an IndieAppStack article is published.

## Reader Value

- The article answers one clear reader job.
- The first section gives a useful short answer.
- The article explains who should choose each option and who should avoid it.
- There is at least one example scenario for a solo mobile developer.

## Evidence

- Claims are checked against `docs/content-affiliate/evidence-log.md` and the Notion Editorial Evidence Log.
- Pricing and feature claims have a source-check date.
- Tool facts come from admin data, official pages, docs, or hands-on notes.
- Hands-on language is supported by notes or removed.
- Open questions are captured in review notes.

## Visuals

- Hero visual is relevant to the article, not generic decoration.
- At least one in-article visual helps the reader decide or act.
- Visuals are tracked in `docs/content-affiliate/visual-production-workflow.md` and the Notion Visual Production Board.
- Screenshots are either owned, captured during real review, or clearly allowed.
- Alt text describes the image meaningfully.

## SEO And Internal Links

- Target keyword and search intent are clear.
- Title, SEO title, meta description, and slug match the intent.
- Article links to at least three relevant internal pages where possible.
- Related categories, tools, comparisons, and Stack Finder are connected naturally.

## Affiliate And Compliance

- Affiliate disclosure appears before or near monetized links.
- Affiliate CTAs are relevant to the section.
- Links use `/go/[slug]` when tracking is needed.
- `sponsored nofollow` is used for monetized links unless stricter rel values apply.
- The article would still be useful without affiliate links.

## Final Decision

Publish only when:

- Human reviewed is true.
- Source checks are current.
- Internal links are present.
- Visual requirements are met or explicitly deferred.
- Affiliate status is correct.
- Review notes do not contain blocking issues.
