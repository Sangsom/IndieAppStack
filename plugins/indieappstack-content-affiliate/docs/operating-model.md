# IndieAppStack Content Affiliate Operating Model

## Mission

Make IndieAppStack the practical field guide for solo mobile developers choosing tools, building small systems, and shipping apps with less wasted motion.

The site should earn trust before it earns commission. Affiliate links are allowed when they help a reader act on a good recommendation, but the article must remain useful if every affiliate link is removed.

## Team Roles

- Content strategist: owns pillars, topic scoring, publishing cadence, and refresh priorities.
- Editorial writer: turns topics into briefs, drafts, visuals, tables, examples, and review notes.
- Affiliate operator: verifies programs, prepares applications, manages link placement, and checks disclosures.
- Performance analyst: reviews traffic, clicks, newsletter, Stack Finder, and affiliate data to steer the next sprint.

## Weekly Cadence

- Monday: choose topics and evidence needed.
- Tuesday to Wednesday: draft one flagship article and one supporting article.
- Thursday: human review, source checks, images, internal links, and disclosure.
- Friday: publish or schedule, then review analytics and update the topic queue.

## Content Mix

- 40% practical guides and setup recipes.
- 25% comparison pages.
- 20% tool reviews or tool clusters.
- 10% launch checklists and templates.
- 5% opinionated field notes or data-backed essays.

## Non-Negotiables

- No fake hands-on testing.
- No unsupported pricing or commission claims.
- No affiliate-first rankings.
- No generic stock imagery.
- No publishing without internal links and source-check dates.

## Existing Product Surface To Use

The repo already includes:

- Admin topic queue.
- AI draft flow.
- Article quality checklist.
- Affiliate program records.
- Affiliate link records and `/go/[slug]` redirect tracking.
- Compliance/disclosure components.
- Click analytics admin pages.
- Plausible event wrapper.

This plugin should feed and improve those surfaces, not bypass them.
