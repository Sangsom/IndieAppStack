# IndieAppStack Article Production

Use this skill when turning a topic into an article brief or draft.

## Article Standard

Every publishable article should include:

- Clear search-intent answer in the introduction.
- Reader persona and use case.
- Decision framework, setup checklist, or comparison table.
- Best-for and not-good-for guidance.
- Internal links to relevant tools, categories, comparisons, and guides.
- Source-check notes for pricing and product claims.
- Image brief for hero and in-article visuals.
- Affiliate disclosure if links are present or planned.
- Human review notes.

## Visual Standard

Prefer owned visuals:

- Stack maps.
- Decision trees.
- Annotated workflow diagrams.
- Screenshots captured during real hands-on review.
- Simple comparison cards or tables.

Avoid generic stock imagery. Do not imply hands-on testing through screenshots unless testing actually happened.

## Draft Guardrails

- Do not invent pricing, feature limits, test results, or affiliate terms.
- Do not rank by commission.
- Do not use "best overall" unless the article explains the constraints.
- Use "choose X when..." language.
- Keep drafts in review until a human verifies claims.

## Output

For a brief, return:

- Title, slug, target keyword, intent, buyer stage, and angle.
- Outline.
- Tools and sources to verify.
- Visual requirements.
- Affiliate/link plan.
- QA checklist.

For a draft, return Markdown plus review notes.
